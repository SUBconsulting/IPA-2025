Evaluating and Scaling the mEducation Program

The mEducation program combines SMS-based math problems with personalized weekly phone tutorials to strengthen students' math skills. After showing promise in Botswana, IPA evaluated the program's effectiveness in the Philippines through a randomized evaluation as part of a multi-country study. The evaluation found that participants improved their math skills by 40 percent compared to students who did not receive the program, at a cost of USD $12 per child. Based on these results, IPA is providing technical assistance to support the program's expansion.

The Challenge

Despite rising global school attendance, many students continue to struggle with low learning levels, highlighting the need for more effective education strategies. The COVID-19 pandemic further exposed gaps in learning, particularly in regions where school disruptions are frequent. To address this, Youth Impact's mEducation program offers a phone-based tutoring solution focused on foundational math skills.

The Intervention

Youth Impact's mEducation program (also known as ConnectEd) is a phone-based tutoring initiative designed to enhance math skills through personalized learning support. The program delivers weekly SMS exercises combined with one-on-one phone tutoring, ensuring instruction aligns with each student’s level.

How it Works:

  • SMS Exercises → Parents receive weekly math exercises via SMS to practice with their child.

  • Phone Calls → Tutors provide weekly guidance through scheduled phone calls.

  • Targeted Instruction → Exercises are tailored to match each student’s learning level.

The Evaluation

In the Philippines, 3,492 students participated in a randomized evaluation, divided into three groups:

  • Control Group → Did not receive the program.

  • SMS + Phone Calls Group → Received both components of the program.

  • SMS-Only Group → Received only text-based exercises.

Additionally, the program was implemented by both the Philippines Department of Education (DepEd) and IPA to evaluate its feasibility for government-led expansion and its impact on learning outcomes.

Results

The full mEducation program (SMS + phone calls) in the Philippines led to a significant 40 percent increase in children’s learning outcomes. The Philippines was among the countries with the biggest impact. The SMS-only intervention led to a 8 percent increase in learning outcomes, but only in the Philippines and Uganda, suggesting that text messages can be effective in contexts with the greatest need. Government-led implementation of mEducation was equally effective as NGO-led implementation.

Cost-Effectiveness

At $12 per child, the program yields 3.4 learning-adjusted years of schooling (LAYS) per $100, making it highly cost-effective.

Policy Implications and Impact

With strong evidence supporting mEducation’s effectiveness, IPA is now focused on expanding the program in partnership with DepEd. By working closely with School Division Offices (SDOs), IPA is helping integrate the program into local education systems, ensuring more students benefit from its approach.

Early results are promising. A March 2024 pilot across 90 schools, 265 teachers, and 1,015 students showed a significant impact:

  • The percentage of students unable to solve basic operations dropped from 56 percent to 15 percent.

  • The share of students solving division problems increased from 2 percent to 17 percent.
